Star Wars RPG Sample Characters

A Complete Guide with Over 100 Ready-to-Play Characters from a Galaxy Far, Far Away

Understanding Star Wars RPG Characters

The Star Wars RPG (using Fantasy Flight Games' system) creates characters through a unique combination of Species, Career, and Specializations. Unlike traditional class-based systems, Star Wars RPG allows for incredible flexibility while maintaining the iconic archetypes that make the galaxy far, far away so compelling.

graph TD A[Species] --> D[Base Characteristics] B[Career] --> E[Career Skills] C[Specialization] --> F[Talent Trees] D --> G[Character Foundation] E --> G F --> G G --> H[Motivation & Obligation] H --> I[Complete Character] I --> J[Force Sensitivity] I --> K[Equipment & Relationships]

The Three Pillars of Character Creation

Every Star Wars character is built on three core elements:

  • Species: Your biological heritage - Human, Twi'lek, Wookiee, etc.
  • Career: Your background and general profession - Smuggler, Diplomat, Bounty Hunter
  • Specialization: Your specific focus within that career - Pilot, Ambassador, Assassin

Real-World Analogy

Think of Star Wars character creation like choosing a profession in our world. Your species is like your cultural background - it influences how you see the world and what advantages you might have. Your career is your general field - like "military" or "entertainment." Your specialization is your specific job within that field - like "fighter pilot" or "musician." Just as a military communications specialist and a military medic have different skills despite the same career background, Star Wars specializations provide focused abilities within broader career paths.

The Role of the Force

Force sensitivity is a special characteristic that can be added to any character concept. Force-sensitive characters have access to Force powers and can develop along Jedi or Sith paths, but they can also be smugglers, diplomats, or any other career who happen to have a connection to the Force.

Character Creation Process

Creating a Star Wars character involves balancing mechanical effectiveness with compelling narrative hooks that fit the space opera genre.

flowchart LR A[Choose Species] --> B[Select Career] B --> C[Pick Specialization] C --> D[Allocate Characteristics] D --> E[Spend Skill Points] E --> F[Choose Starting Talents] F --> G[Determine Motivation] G --> H[Calculate Obligation/Duty] H --> I[Select Equipment] I --> J[Force Rating Optional] J --> K[Final Character]

When to Use These Sample Characters

  • New Players: Jump into the Star Wars universe without overwhelming character creation
  • One-Shots: Quick character selection for convention games or movie marathons
  • Campaign Starters: Pre-made groups with built-in relationships and motivations
  • NPCs: Ready-made supporting characters and antagonists
  • Inspiration: Templates for creating custom characters with similar themes

Era Considerations

These characters are designed for flexibility across different Star Wars eras:

  • Prequel Era: Republic at its height, Jedi Order active, Clone Wars
  • Original Trilogy Era: Imperial oppression, Rebellion, hidden Jedi
  • Sequel Era: New Republic, First Order, emerging Force users
  • High Republic: Jedi at their peak, exploration and discovery

The Hero's Journey in Star Wars

Star Wars follows classic mythological patterns that these characters embody:

  • Call to Adventure: Characters' obligations and duties pull them into larger conflicts
  • Mentor Figures: Older, wiser characters guide younger ones
  • Trials and Tests: Personal growth through facing fears and challenges
  • Return Transformed: Characters emerge changed by their experiences

Destiny and the Force

Even non-Force characters participate in the galaxy's destiny:

  • Destiny Points: Flip them to represent the Force flowing through events
  • Meaningful Coincidences: "Chance" encounters that drive the story forward
  • Moral Choices: Decisions that tip the balance between Light and Dark
  • Legacy Building: Actions that influence future generations

Faction Dynamics

Star Wars thrives on the tension between competing factions:

  • Ideological Conflicts: Freedom vs Order, Republic vs Empire
  • Resource Competition: Control of hyperspace routes, planets, technology
  • Personal Betrayals: Characters switching sides for personal reasons
  • Unlikely Alliances: Former enemies working together against greater threats
